First of all, thank you very much for spending your time helping us review the works submitted to _Playing futures_. In this page you have the basic instructions for reviewing. It will be updated to address any questions we receive.

Submissions to _Playing Futures_ are expected to be work in progress, breaking work, or experimental and creative proposals. As such, the main criteria for acceptance is that the paper contributes with new ideas, and that these ideas are presented clearly and productively. We want to accept works that are not only good in themselves, but that can also create an interesting conversation. In case of doubt, err on the side of experiments and risk-taking.

We would like the reviews to be brief and to the point, so we expect them to be between the 400 and 800 words, but this is just our suggestion, and not a rule. Remember to be constructive on the feedback, so that the authors can use it to sharpen their arguments, and make for better presentations and future contributions. 

When reviewing the creative, interactive works, it is as important to evaluate the (probably subjective) quality of the piece as it is to see how the creators will talk about them. _Playing Futures_ is not an exhibition, and while we will try to make it possible to play with the games and playable media that is submitted, the presentation by the creators is what matters the most. So balance your judgement between the quality of the work and the eloquence of the creators in writing about it.

All abstracts and works will have two reviewers, and Miguel Sicart will be the meta-reviewer in charge of taking the final decision [^1]. All reviews will be made available to the authors, but there will be no rebuttal period.

Again, than you so much for your time, and for helping us make _Playing Futures_ a successful experiment.

[^1]: Yes, this is weird. In the future, we expect _Playing Futures_ to be a "proper" conference, with committees and tracks and chairs and other types of academic furniture. But this first time, we're doing it a bit differently. For any and all complains about this decision, you can [write to Miguel](mailto:miguel@itu.dk).
